> **Where am I?** The warm channel. The Lead-Researcher next door works cold: find strangers, qualify,
> draft outreach. This folder is the opposite motion. It does not hunt. It **remembers** the people who
> already trust Josh, gives them something real first, and lets a paid engagement grow out of a

## The one rule
**You do not ask a friend for money. You give first, then let the conversation become whatever it
becomes.** The ask, if it comes, comes from them or comes last. Trust is the asset; do not spend it
on a pitch.

## Trust ledger, not CRM
A CRM tracks a funnel of strangers. A ledger tracks relationships that already exist and the value you
can hand each person first. Per contact, four lines and nothing more (template:
`trust-ledger-vorlage.md`):
- **Who** and the real nature of the trust (former colleague, climbing friend, ex-mentor's network).
- **Value I can hand them first** with no strings: a read on their AI mess, an intro, a short walk-through. Not a pitch.
- **The honest reason it's them.** If you cannot name it, they are not warm, they are convenient.
- **Next human-gated touch:** one low-friction, curious message, drafted, awaiting Josh's yes.

## The loop (warm, not cold)
```
W1_erinnern   → Name people who already trust Josh. Fill the ledger. → ergebnisse/
W2_geben      → For each, the one specific thing to give first, no strings.
W3_einladen   → One human, curious touch. "Vorschlag, kein Pitch." Offer a free pilot where it fits.  🚪 Josh sends
W4_vertiefen  → The conversation becomes a pilot, a referral, or a paid scope. Capture the story.       🚪 Josh decides
```
One person, one touch, at a time. A warm conversation that actually happens beats a cold sequence that ships. The metric is **real conversations**, not messages sent.

## Free pilots are the entry door (on purpose)
Josh may do some free work to get practice and get the word out. That is a strategy, not a discount.
A small, time-boxed, real piece of ICM work, given to a warm contact, buys three things cold outreach
cannot: **practice** (reps before the stakes are high), a **testimonial / case study** (the proof the
field said is missing), and **word of mouth** (the warm network's referral engine). The boutique
research is blunt about this: the deliverable becomes the portfolio piece for the next client. Frame
it honestly as a pilot with a clear edge, not "free forever." Details and the offer language:
`freie-pilotprojekte.md`.

## The human gate
Every outward touch is Josh's explicit yes. This folder drafts, lists, and proposes. It never sends,
never books, never promises. Warm relationships are higher-trust and therefore higher-stakes: a clumsy
message to a friend costs more than a bounced cold email. Gate holds, always.

## How this connects to the rest of the system
- **The Coach** defaults here. Its warm-channel bias and Pattern Ledger point at this folder; when the
  Coach picks "one warm move," this is where it lives.
- **The Lead-Researcher** is the cold counterpart. Same human gate, opposite motion. Do not mix the
  two pipelines or their results.
- **A pilot that converts** graduates to `../pipeline/` or becomes a new folder in `../../clients/`.
- **Marketing** turns a finished pilot into a story (case study, post). The asset is the proof.
