# Free Pilots: the honest entry offer

> Josh may do some free work to land the first paid yes. This file keeps that honest and useful, so a
> free pilot earns proof and practice instead of quietly devaluing the work.

## Why free, on purpose
A free pilot is a strategy, not a discount. Given to a warm contact, a small real piece of ICM work buys
three things that money-first cold outreach cannot:
1. **Practice.** Reps with the method before the stakes are high. Josh has never sold his own work; the
   pilot is where the muscle is built.
2. **Proof.** A testimonial and a (anonymized) case study. The Terroir read flagged the missing proof as
   a real gap. A finished pilot closes it.
3. **Word of mouth.** The warm network refers. One happy pilot in a trusted circle travels further than
   forty cold emails.

The boutique research backs the mechanism: the deliverable becomes the portfolio piece for the next
client. Ship one real thing, well, then let it sell the next.

## What keeps it from devaluing the work
- **Time-boxed and scoped.** One workflow, one team, one clear deliverable, a named end date. Not "I'll
  help with your AI." A pilot with edges.
- **Named as a pilot, with a why.** "I am taking on two pilots to build case studies before I price
  this." Honest framing protects the future price. Free-forever does not exist here.
- **A clear exchange, even without money.** In return: a short testimonial, permission to write it up
  (anonymized if needed), and an intro to one person who could use this. The exchange is explicit and agreed up front.
- **An on-ramp to paid.** The pilot ends with a concrete option: "Here is what a paid next step would
  look like." No pressure, but the door is named.

## The shape of a good first pilot
- Small enough to finish in days, not months (speed is part of the value).
- Real enough to produce a result the contact actually feels.
- Inside ICM's lane: making the AI they already own usable, with the people kept at the center.
- Documentable: something Marketing can turn into a story the moment it ships.

## Honest cautions
- Two or three pilots, not ten. Free work has a real cost: Josh's time and focus, which the field
  already flagged as the bottleneck. Cap it.
- A pilot for someone who would never pay is practice, not pipeline. Be clear which one it is in the ledger.
- Keep claims honest in the pilot too: ICM helps ask better questions and keeps humans in control. No
  sweeping privacy or results guarantees, even for free.

## The gate
Offering a pilot is an outward commitment. It is drafted here and goes out only on Josh's explicit yes.
