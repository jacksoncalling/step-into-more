# Qualification Rubric (Level 3 — Reference Document)

> Applied during the `02_qualify/` stage. Four axes, scored 1–3 points each. The objective is not to collect "many leads," but rather the *right* leads—possessing Power, Pain, and Maturity.

## The Four Axes

| Axis | Metric | 1 | 2 | 3 |
|-------|-------|---|---|---|
| **Power** | Does this person decide on budget/methodology? | Champion/Intel only, no systemic power | Influence, but no budget control | Decision-maker with budget ownership |
| **Pain** | How acute is the problem we solve? | Vague/undefined | Noticeable/felt daily | Critical / Explicitly voiced |
| **Signal** | Indicators of maturity or timing? | None | Soft signal (Specific role, industry trend) | Hard signal (AI initiative, R&D unit, open tender, structural overhaul) |
| **Entry Door** | Is it clear which offer to lead with? | Unclear | Plausible | Obvious (Wedge vs. Consulting→Limina) |

## Segmentation Categories
- **9–12 = Hot** → Move to `03_outreach/` + Main Pipeline.
- **6–8 = Warm** → Monitor / nurture, wait for a harder signal.
- **≤5 = Cold / Champion** → Retain strictly as Intel (to map decision-makers); do not pitch them.

## Vital Rule
An individual experiencing high **Pain** but lacking **Power** is a **Champion**. Nurture this relationship to gain access to the actual economic decision-maker, but do NOT pitch them directly. (GFN trainers and coaches fit exactly into this category.)
