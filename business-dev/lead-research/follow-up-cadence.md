# Follow-up Cadence (Level 3 — Reference Document)

> Applied after a first outreach is sent (Stage 03) and gets no reply. Generic — works for any
> contact in any segment. Goal: stay respectful and proportionate (UWG), keep a human gate on
> every touch, and know when to switch to a second contact at the same org.

## The Rhythm (per contact, no reply)

| When | Action | Content |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Day 0** | First outreach (Stage 03) | The tailored, signal-anchored draft. 🚪 Josh sends. |
| **Day +7** | Follow-up #1 — same person | Short value-add: one concrete item (one-pager, 2-min walkthrough). **Never** "nur kurz nachgefragt". 🚪 Josh sends. |
| **Day +14** | Follow-up #2 — "close the loop" | Soft breakup note ("ich lasse es für jetzt dabei – melden Sie sich gern, wenn es später passt"). Often triggers a reply. 🚪 Josh sends. |
| **After Day +14** | Pivot to a *second* contact at the same org | **Fresh angle, not a re-send.** Lead with the new person's distinct pain. Light transparency is fine ("ich hatte mich an … gewandt") — small orgs talk. |

## Rules
- **Max 2–3 touches** to a non-responder, then stop or pivot. (UWG proportionality.)
- **Honor any opt-out / objection instantly** — record it on the card, no further contact (UWG/DSGVO).
- **Multi-channel beats single.** A LinkedIn touch between emails lifts reply rates — professional, public channels only.
- **One reply ends the cadence.** A reply routes to `04_antwort/`; stop the scheduled follow-ups.
- **Don't pivot to a second contact too early.** One week of silence means "busy," not "no."

## Where the state lives
- Track the next-touch date on the contact card (`Outreach Execution` → Next Step / a Follow-up log).
- The board shows the contact in **Sent**; it does not auto-advance until a reply (→ Replied) or a meeting (→ Meeting Set).
- `[MCP later]` — the Gmail MCP can surface "no reply after 7 days" and draft the follow-up; **sending stays a 🚪 human gate.**
