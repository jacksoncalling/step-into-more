# Compliance & Confidentiality Checklist (Level 3 — Reference)

> Run this before any draft is marked **send-ready** (end of Stage 03; also for follow-ups in
> 04/05). It converts judgment into a checklist so the operator stays safe on **any model tier**.
> If a box can't be ticked, the draft is NOT send-ready — fix it, or flag it for Josh.

## A. Confidentiality (cross-client)
- [ ] No other client named or implied. (Esp. **DAA / "Talentcenter" / "TCAK"** must never appear in another org's outreach.)
- [ ] No reference to work done for a different client ("für X habe ich … entwickelt").
- [ ] Any doubt about whether something is client-confidential → **ask Josh, don't send.**

## B. Cold-email legality (UWG §7)
- [ ] **Business contact only** — professional role, business address. No private individuals/addresses.
- [ ] **Offer is plausibly relevant** to this person's professional responsibility.
- [ ] **Sender clearly identified** — name, company, email, phone (signature block present).
- [ ] **Opt-out / objection line present** ("…sagen Sie kurz Bescheid, dann melde ich mich nicht erneut.").
- [ ] **Frequency proportionate** — within `follow-up-cadence.md` limits (max 2–3 touches).

## C. Data protection (DSGVO)
- [ ] Contact data came from a **public corporate channel** (Impressum, website, public listing).
- [ ] **No scraping of private/personal profiles** for data.
- [ ] **Pattern-guessed business addresses are OK** (e.g. `vorname.nachname@firma.de`) — these are corporate, not private, channels. Mark the address **"Muster, unbestätigt"** on the card; a bounce is acceptable, harmless feedback. (Still prefer a verified address or `info@ "z. Hd."` when one exists.)
- [ ] Any opt-out already received is honored — contact removed.

## D. Honest claims (no sweeping guarantees)
- [ ] No absolute legal promises ("vollkommen DSGVO-/EU-AI-Act-konform").
- [ ] Compliance framed as **approach + questions**, not a guarantee ("lässt sich datensparsam aufsetzen…").
- [ ] Data-privacy points are accurate and modest. A real pilot involves the client's DSB.

## D2. Reads as human-written (no AI tells)
- [ ] **No em-dashes (—) or en-dashes (–) anywhere** in subject, body, or signature. They are the clearest sign AI wrote it. Use commas, periods, parentheses, or restructure the sentence. (Normal German compound hyphens like „KI-gestützt" are fine; those are Bindestriche, not the tell.)
- [ ] No piled-up "nicht X, sondern Y" or rule-of-three patterns; sentence length varies; no buzzword polish. Read it aloud: if it sounds machine-smooth, roughen it.

## E. Substance
- [ ] **Signal-anchored** — one specific, verified, timely reference (not generic).
- [ ] **Right door** for the segment (ICM-Keil for KMU; Beratung→Limina for Träger; **never Limina cold**).
- [ ] **No invented data** — unconfirmed details marked "unbestätigt".

> All boxes ticked → mark the card **send-ready**, 🚪 hand to Josh. Any box blank → fix or flag.
