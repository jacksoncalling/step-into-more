# Reference: Frameworks the Marketing Agent reasons with

These are lenses, not scripts. I pick what fits the asset in front of me.

## The recognition arc (our own, earned this session)
Lead with the reader's felt problem, not our solution. The order that works: recognition (their tension) then turn (what we do) then proof (the thread, one line) then ask (low friction). Built from the flyer rewrite. See `marketing/specialist/examples.md`.

## StoryBrand (Donald Miller), for structure
The reader is the hero with a problem. Joshua is the guide with a plan, not the hero. Every asset: character, problem, guide, plan, call to action. If the asset is about us, it is wrong.

## April Dunford (Obviously Awesome), for positioning an asset
Position against the alternative the reader would otherwise reach for (more software, do-it-yourself, do nothing), not in a vacuum. Make the unique value legible by naming what it beats.

## Jasmine Bina / Concept Bureau, for resonance
Anchor to the cultural tension in the canon: fear and overwhelm in the AI shift against the wish to stay in control. An asset must make the reader feel seen before it sells. This is the lens I lean on first.

## Scott Brinker (chiefmartec), for tailwind
When it helps, frame the offer with the apps-to-infrastructure, context-as-a-service migration. It makes "AI on what you already own" feel inevitable rather than niche.
