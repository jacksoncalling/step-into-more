# Boutique Marketing Playbook (research-grounded)

> How small, distinctive consultancies actually win, synthesized from research on boutique and solo
> AI consulting marketing (2026) and mapped onto Step Into More. The Marketing actor reads this to
> stay grounded in real patterns, not generic brochure instincts. Sources at the bottom.

## The one thing the research keeps saying
**Differentiation is focus, not buzzwords.** The consulting world is full of sameness. You win by
knowing exactly where you fit and having the nerve to say where you don't. Trying to appeal to
"companies that need help" makes you invisible. The narrower the niche, the faster the brand grows.

For us: we are not "an AI consultant." We help **educational providers and SMEs use the AI they
already own, with the people kept at the center.** Name the fit. Name the not-fit (we are not the
people who sell you a new platform or promise compliance magic).

## How boutiques win (and what it means for us)
1. **Personal brand + referrals beat scaled inbound.** Boutiques grow through trust and word of
   mouth, not channel volume. → Marketing's job is to make warm conversations easier, not to chase
   reach. Assets are doors for the warm channel, not a megaphone. (See `business-dev/warm-network/`.)
2. **A point of view that takes a stand.** Thought leadership that builds reputation is specific,
   takes a position, and is occasionally uncomfortable. "Contrarian and right." Rooted in real
   experience, never repurposed advice. → Josh already holds contrarian POVs: anti-hype, **Ruhe
   statt FOMO**, sovereignty over lock-in, human agency over automation, embodiment as a lens on AI.
   Stop softening these. They ARE the differentiation.
3. **A curated newsletter to a small list** outperforms broad multi-channel marketing, with less
   effort. → The Substack long game is the right instrument. Quality of list over size.
4. **Clients buy outcomes, confidence, and peace of mind**, not "25% efficiency." → Translate
   ICM into the reader's stakes (their team's calm, their data staying theirs), never feature lists.
5. **Speed and proof as the product.** AI-augmented consultants ship in days, and each deliverable
   becomes the portfolio piece for the next client. → This is the engine behind free/pilot work:
   do one real thing fast, well, in public; let it sell the next one. "Proof over talk."
6. **Memorable, owned terminology.** Distinctive frameworks and language travel. → We already own
   some: the **golden thread** (Solar to Sovereignty), **guide at the edge**, the **ICM wedge**,
   the **"memory" hook**, **time between worlds**. Use them consistently; do not reinvent per asset.

## The contrarian POV bank (Josh's, ready to stand on)
Each of these is a stand worth taking publicly, in his real voice. Uncomfortable on purpose.
- "You do not need new AI software. You need to actually use the one you already pay for."
- "AI urgency is a sales tactic. Calm is a competitive advantage."
- "If the tool forgets you after every conversation, it is not your colleague, it is a stranger."
- "Sovereignty is not a feature. It is whether the knowledge stays yours."
- "We learned to read solar systems on rooftops at night. AI adoption is the same: you measure
  the real thing in the real conditions, with the people who live there."
- "The body knows before the dashboard does. Physical intelligence belongs in the AI conversation."

## What this changes about how Marketing works
- Lead with a stand, then the recognition, then the proof, then the door. A safe asset is a failed asset.
- Prefer the small, sharp, owned (newsletter, one essay, one pilot story) over the broad and generic.
- Measure assets by whether they open a real conversation, not by impressions.
- Free/pilot work gets documented as a story the moment it ships. The case study is the asset.

## Honest caveats
- Contrarian only works if it is true and lived. Do not manufacture edgy takes. Each POV above is
  anchored in Josh's actual experience (solar, carpentry, movement, the AI transition he is living).
- Boutique pricing research (outcomes over hours, deliverable-as-portfolio) is US-leaning. The German
  Bildungsträger / KMU market is slower and trust-first. Keep the playbook, adjust the tempo.
