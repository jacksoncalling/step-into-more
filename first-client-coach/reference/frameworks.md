# Reference: Frameworks the Coach reasons with

Lenses for discovery, qualification, positioning, and distribution. Not scripts. I pick what fits the moment (see `identity.md`).

## How to ask (discovery dialogue)
- **SPIN (Neil Rackham).** Situation, Problem, Implication, Need-payoff. The consultative question arc. Move a conversation from "what is" to "what it costs" to "what a fix is worth."
- **The Mom Test (Rob Fitzpatrick).** Ask about real past behavior and specifics, never "would you buy this." Do not lead. The ethic that keeps discovery honest, with Joshua and with prospects.
- **JTBD Switch / Forces of Progress (Bob Moesta).** Push, pull, anxiety, habit. Why would someone "hire" Limina, Terroir, or the consulting, and what holds them back?

## How to position (the story)
- **April Dunford (Obviously Awesome).** Position against the alternative the prospect would otherwise reach for (more software, do-it-yourself, do nothing), not in a vacuum. Name what we beat.
- **Jasmine Bina / Concept Bureau.** Anchor to the cultural tension (canon): fear and overwhelm in the AI shift against the wish to stay in control.
- **Scott Brinker (chiefmartec).** Read where the field is migrating (apps to infrastructure, context-as-a-service) and use it as tailwind: "AI on what you already own" is the direction of travel, not a niche.

## How to choose a channel (distribution)
- **Bullseye, from Traction (Weinberg & Mares).** Test a few channels, watch which produce conversations, concentrate there. The starting bullseye is the three: warm network, local cold drops, targeted cold email.

## How to qualify (whom to pursue)
- We do not keep our own rubric. We use the Lead-Researcher's, because it is battle-tested. See `inherited.md`.
- **GPCT** (HubSpot) for the discovery sequence; **MEDDIC** when a bigger partner brings a buying committee. Both feed the rubric's Power and Signal axes.
