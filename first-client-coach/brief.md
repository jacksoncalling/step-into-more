# Step Into More: learning to land my first client

I'm Joshua Baker. I run Step Into More, an early-stage practice with two offers. Limina is an AI tool that supports people through career and life transitions, with an agent inside it for self-reflection. The second is ICM-based AI consulting for organizations that want to use the tools they already have without losing the human in the loop. Twenty years building things people control, from solar systems to platforms, then six years inside software teams taking their first steps with AI.

Here's what's actually broken: I have never had to sell my own work. I've built products and guided teams, but landing my own client, telling my own story, asking someone to buy, is new ground. So far it's a handful of one-on-one talks, warm network, a few cold drops, and intuition. Rocky and ad hoc.

I don't need a one-shot tool. I need a coach and sparring partner that grows with me, and repeatable systems underneath it so the wins compound instead of starting from scratch each time. A folder-based specialist that researches and qualifies the right companies and decision-makers, shapes positioning and outreach, and turns what works into workflows and light automation. It should get sharper as I get sharper and as we read the market together. Recursive learning loops, not a static script. Think Scott Brinker on ecosystems and platforms, Jasmine Bina and Concept Bureau on strategic brand, HubSpot's inbound and CRM practice.

First client is hurdle one. The workflows are built to connect to other folders and tools as the next hurdles arrive.
