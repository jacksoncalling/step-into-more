## What we do (one line)
The hook that lands, straight from the flyer and validated in person (a print-shop worker, unprompted: "oh ja, das Problem habe ich"):

**Ihre KI vergisst Sie und gibt generische Antworten. Wir geben ihr ein Gedächtnis aus dem Wissen Ihrer Menschen, mit den Werkzeugen, die Sie schon nutzen, mit dem Menschen an jedem Schritt.**

In plain English: we build on what your people already know and give the AI a memory, so it knows your organization instead of forgetting you. The word that makes people lean in is "memory" (Gedächtnis). Lead with it.

## The cultural tension (Bina lens)
The human knot inside the AI shift: fear, overwhelm, and the worry of being replaced, set against the wish to stay in control and grow. We do not sell over that fear. We guide through it. Education, transparency, respect.

## How we are positioned (Dunford + Brinker)
Not another AI app. A method that turns tools you already own (Microsoft 365, ChatGPT, Claude) into AI that knows your organization. ICM is interpretable context: a method, not a platform, no lock-in. Brinker's current thesis, apps to infrastructure and "context-as-a-service," is the tailwind: we are infrastructure on what you already have, not one more app. The alternatives we displace: buying more software, do-it-yourself chaos, or doing nothing.

## The golden thread
Solar to Platforms to Coaching to Sovereignty. Twenty years giving people control: first over their own energy, now over their own intelligence. "Aus dem Wissen Ihrer Menschen." The knowledge belongs to the people, not the machine.
